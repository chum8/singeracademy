Mr. Douglas Michael Singer is currently a freelance piano teacher in Hastings, NE, and the CEO of SingTeam(TM) America, a historically unprecedented opportunity that unites creative, motivated, and ingenious students, who sing expressively and use their gifts of dance, sales, leadership, and spirit, to form teams that seek to win competitions, awards, and the SingTeam(TM) championship.

Mr. Singer has enjoyed playing piano for 22 years and has taught for 10 years. A native of Colorado, Mr. Singer received degrees in music from Gardner-Webb University (B.M., 2004) and the University of Northern Colorado (M.M., 2006).

Mr. Singer taught piano and accompanied piano in Indiana for 4 years, serving as an adjunct piano professor at Taylor University, Ranked #1 Baccalaureate College in the Midwest for 5 years running. He appeared as a faculty soloist, feature artist, and ensemble pianist at various Taylor University events, and gave a joint concert in conjunction with mezzo-soprano Jennifer Arbogast. He played jazz piano with the Jazz Rolle Sextet. He formed a jazz combo and directed jazz vocalists for the Taylor Sounds Valentine's Banquet.

Mr. Singer was commissioned by Shakespeare Playworks of Muncie, IN, to write original music for their 2012 production of "As You Like It." Mr. Singer became the Music Director for the production, recruiting a rock n' roll band and directing the vocalists for the production. Shakespeare Playworks plans to have Mr. Singer return as Composer and Music Director for their 2013 season.

As a former collaborative pianist in the studios of Mei Zhong of Ball State University, Patricia Robertson of Taylor University, Shederick Whipple of Taylor University, and others, Mr. Singer accompanied numerous award winning vocalists. He accompanied a three concert series by mezzo-soprano Loralee Songer, including a presentation at the College Music Society Great Lakes Regional Conference. He accompanied the winning performances of two NATS Advanced Division First Place vocalists, the Canadian soprano Julie Lyn Barber and the Korean baritone Yeong-June Choi. He accompanied other award-winning performances at Indiana NATS and MTNA. He accompanied and coached numerous students at Taylor University, and served as accompanist for Taylor Theater, Taylor Sounds, Taylor Chorale, Taylor Opera, and Taylor Adoration Chorus. He played piano for the Farmland Dinner Theater performance of “Clue: The Musical.”

Mr. Singer appeared with the Marion Philharmonic Orchestra as Keyboard II player during the May 2010 performance of Stravinsky's “Symphony of the Psalms” under the baton of Alex Platt. He appeared as piano soloist during the world premiere of his original work, “Judah,” premiered by Patricia Sparti and the Gardner-Webb University Community Orchestra.

Douglas Singer is married to long-time Hastings resident and Hastings College Graduate, Laura Singer (nee Demick). They have two beautiful children: a son Edmund, age 2, and their infant daughter Lavinia.
